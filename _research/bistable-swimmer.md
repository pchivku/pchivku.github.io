We developed an underactuated fish-like robot with a passive bistable tail. The design combines a hydrofoil with a double-well elastic tail, controlled by one internal rotor.

<video autoplay muted loop playsinline width="800"><source src="/files/Bistable_rotor_small.mp4" type="video/mp4"></video>

By tuning input amplitude and frequency, the robot switches between straight swimming and powered turning around a deflected tail position. It reaches 2.1 body lengths per second.
